The Vigenère cipher

While not being used in World War II, the Vigenère cipher is an excellent example of a cipher more difficult to crack. This cipher requires text, not a number, for a key. It is essentially multiple Caesar ciphers, but with the key changing for each letter of the message. The cipher was created by Giovan Battista Bellaso, but it gets its name from Blaise de Vigenère, another cryptographer, due to the cipher being popularly misattributed to him.
