Decrypting the Enigma

Like the Vigenère, the Enigma is difficult to decrypt because it involves a great many settings. There are over fifteen billion billion possible keys for the Engima, which resulted in it taking two years for the Allies to decrypt. Due to the complexity of the Enigma, there is no example on this page. However, you likely were unable to find the key for the Vigenère cipher. Decoding the Enigma was even more difficult than the Vigenère because the Allies didn't know how the Enigma was encoded, only how to input a key and a message and receive deciphered text. Decoding ciphers with many possible keys is extremely challenging, and the Enigma could only be decoded by analyzing frequency of words.
